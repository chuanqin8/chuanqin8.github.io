About

Hi! I am Chuan Qin (秦川), a Ph.D. candidate at the School of Information Science and Engineering, Lanzhou University, advised by Prof. Long Jin.

My research sits at the intersection of deep learning theory and embodied intelligence. I develop optimization algorithms that improve the generalization and efficiency of neural networks, and I apply these theoretical insights to robot control and decision-making. I am particularly interested in bridging dynamical systems theory and modern deep learning — understanding how training dynamics, sharpness-aware minimization, and gradient flow influence the generalization of large models. I believe that a principled understanding of optimization landscapes is key to building robust, efficient, and generalizable AI systems. I was deeply involved in projects funded by CCF-Huawei, CCF-Baidu, and CAAI-Huawei MindSpore. My work has appeared in Neural Networks, IEEE Transactions on Consumer Electronics, and IEEE/CAA Journal of Automatica Sinica. I serve as a reviewer for IEEE/CAA Journal of Automatica Sinica (JAS).

In my spare time, I am a half-marathon runner. Personal best: 1:42:38 at the 2024 Lanzhou Marathon.
